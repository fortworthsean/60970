Conclusion

Native Americans profoundly shaped colonial life, and the culture of what we know today as Latin America. They left their influence on colonial governments, urban development, society, and economic structures. These impacts are vividly seen throughout the survival or transformation of spatial, social, and naming practices that defined the colonial era.
