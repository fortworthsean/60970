Society

A picture representing colonial society.

Colonial society was a blend of colonial settlers, Native Americans, and enslaved African populations, leading to many adjustments in societal hierarchies. Native languages sometimes became the predominant spoken language in a community, especially in areas with large Indigenous populations. Furthermore, Native farming and cuisine shaped community diets and marketplaces. Imagine what modern diets would be like without corn or tomatoes, both of which are products of Native American farming.
